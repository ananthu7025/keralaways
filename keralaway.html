Volume Three

Kerala, through the seasons

March — May

Summer.

— Stillness

The state slows. Coastal afternoons spent in mango shade, dawn walks before the heat. The light is honey, the sea is silver.

June — September

Monsoon.

— Awakening

The land exhales. Rivers fill, the green deepens to ink. Old healers say this is the only season worth being treated in. We agree.

October — February

Winter.

— Clarity

Mornings cool enough for a shawl in Munnar. Festival lamps in every village. The backwaters are glass; the tea hills are sharp against the sky.

Field Notes

From the kitchen, slowly.

Kerala's table is not one cuisine but three — written in coconut, tamarind, and the unhurried hand of grandmothers.

"Cooking, like a temple, must take its time."

— overheard in a Travancore kitchen

Pepper coast, biryani country

Malabar

Where Arab dhows once moored. Slow-cooked meats, thalassery biryani, banana-leaf parottas torn by hand.

Coconut, tamarind, restraint

Travancore

The royal kitchen. Avial, olan, the precise sourness of kalan. Cooking that whispers, never shouts.

Backwater catch, daily rice

Central Kerala

Karimeen on banana leaves. Toddy-shop fish curries. The simplest meal eaten with the most attention.
